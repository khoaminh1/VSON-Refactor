The Challenge

A large customer base. A small repeat purchase rate.

The client is a fast-growing Vietnamese fashion and footwear retailer with a national store network and a growing online presence. They had built a sizable customer database over several years. But most customers bought once and didn't come back. The marketing team was running mass broadcast campaigns to the whole list, but open rates were declining and revenue from digital channels remained a fraction of total sales.

The team knew they had something to work with. What they lacked was the segmentation capability and campaign infrastructure to reach the right customers with the right products at the right moment.

The Solution

Purchase-history segmentation. Always-on campaigns that stayed relevant.

VSON activated a segmented digital messaging campaign through Zalo and Viber, targeting customer lists built from purchase history, product category affinity, and recency. Personalised offers were sent at optimal send times per segment. A/B testing on message format and call-to-action helped the team quickly learn what drove clicks and conversions in each audience group.

What we did, step by step

  • Analysed the existing customer database and identified purchase-history segments: active buyers, lapsed buyers (90 days+), and category loyalists (e.g. footwear-only buyers).

  • Designed a short-cycle campaign calendar: new arrival alerts, restocking notifications, and promotional events, mapped to each segment's likely interests.

  • Built Zalo and Viber messaging templates with A/B variants for subject lines, imagery descriptions, and call-to-action wording.

  • Set optimal send-time logic per segment based on past engagement patterns (e.g. evening sends for working-age adults, weekend sends for footwear buyers).

  • Launched the first campaign wave and monitored conversion per segment in real time, reallocating budget to highest-performing audience groups within 48 hours.

  • Handed the team a repeatable campaign playbook so future campaign runs required minimal setup time.
